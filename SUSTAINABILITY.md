## üå± Our Approach to Sustainability

Luminous Dynamics operates on **gift economy principles** with a **seven-year transition to full community ownership**. This document outlines how we sustain our work while honoring ERC values.

## üíö Core Principles

### 1. Gift Economy Over Extraction
We receive contributions as gifts and offer our work as gifts. Financial support is voluntary, not extractive.

### 2. Regenerative Over Exploitative
We build systems that restore and enhance rather than deplete. Our funding model must align with this.

### 3. Community Ownership
We transition from founder-led to community-governed over seven years, ensuring long-term mission alignment.

### 4. Transparency
All funding, expenses, and financial decisions are shared openly with the community.

### 5. Multiple Income Streams
We diversify funding to avoid dependence on any single source that might compromise our mission.

## üíé Seven-Year Transition Plan

**Funding Model**: Primarily donations + early investment

**Goals**:
- Establish sustainable funding streams
- Build financial reserves
- Transparent reporting begins

**Target**: $50K-100K annual budget

### Phase 2: Stewardship (2026-2027)
**Funding Model**: Diversified income streams

**Goals**:
- Grant funding secured
- Service offerings launched

- Reserves for 12 months operations

### Phase 3: Community Governance (2028-2030)
**Funding Model**: Community-directed resources

**Goals**:
- Community controls 50% of budget decisions
- Contributor stipends/fellowships
- Self-sustaining operations
- Reserves for 24 months

**Target**: $500K-1M annual budget

### Phase 4: Sacred Collective (2031+)
**Funding Model**: Fully community-owned

**Goals**:
- 100% community governance of finances
- Sustainable without founder involvement
- Supports contributors at livable wages
- Mission guaranteed in perpetuity

**Target**: Scale appropriate to community needs

## üåä Gift Economy in Practice

### What This Means

**For Contributors**:
- Contributions are gifts, not transactions
- No expectation of payment for most contributions
- Stipends/fellowships offered as capacity allows
- Recognition through non-financial means

**For Users**:
- All software remains free and open source
- Financial support is voluntary
- No features locked behind paywalls
- Sliding scale for any paid services

**For the Organization**:
- Money received as gift to serve mission
- Spending guided by community benefit
- Wealth distributed, not accumulated
- Transparency builds trust

### Challenges We Acknowledge

- Sustainability without traditional revenue models
- Fair compensation for contributors in capitalist economy
- Balancing gift economy with survival needs
- Transitioning donors to community governance

### Our Commitments

- Honest about financial realities
- Creative in finding aligned funding
- Patient with transition timeline
- Transparent when we make mistakes

## ‚ùì Questions About Sustainability

### FAQ

**Q: Will projects always be free and open source?**
A: Yes. This is a core commitment protected by our governance.

**Q: How do you handle growth without compromising values?**
A: Slow, intentional growth. Quality over quantity. Community governance prevents mission drift.

**Q: What if funding falls short?**
A: We scale to available resources. Essential projects are prioritized. Community helps decide trade-offs.

**Q: Can I see exact financial numbers?**
A: Detailed reporting begins Q1 2026. Early stage finances are bootstrap-level.

**Q: How do contributors get compensated?**
A: Currently through recognition and gift economy reciprocity. Stipends/fellowships starting 2026 as resources allow.

**Q: Who makes funding decisions now?**
A: Core team with community input. Transitioning to community governance over seven years.
