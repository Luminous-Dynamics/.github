## üéØ Event Philosophy

### Why Events Matter

**Events create:**
- **Connection** - Meet the humans behind GitHub avatars
- **Learning** - Share knowledge through workshops and talks
- **Collaboration** - Pair programming, hackathons, co-creation
- **Celebration** - Recognize achievements and milestones
- **Belonging** - Strengthen sense of community

### Core Principles

1. **Inclusive by Design**
   - Accessible venues and virtual options
   - Diverse speakers and participants
   - Welcoming to all skill levels
   - Multiple participation modes

2. **Conscious Hosting**
   - Code of Conduct enforced
   - Psychological safety prioritized
   - Sustainable practices (minimal waste)
   - Care for hosts and attendees

3. **Value Over Scale**
   - 10 engaged people > 100 passive attendees
   - Deep connection > broad reach
   - Quality experience > big numbers

4. **Hybrid When Possible**
   - In-person for deep connection
   - Virtual for accessibility and reach
   - Recorded for those who can't attend
   - Documentation for all

## üé≠ Types of Events

### 1. Local Meetups

**Format:** 2-3 hours, 10-50 people, casual

**Structure:**
- **Welcome & Intro** (10 min)
- **Lightning Talks** (30 min) - 3√ó 10-minute talks
- **Break** (15 min) - Networking, snacks
- **Workshop or Discussion** (60 min)
- **Social Time** (30 min)

**Best For:**
- Monthly community building
- Local connections
- Informal learning

**Budget:** $100-500 (venue, food)

### 2. Hackathons

**Format:** 1-2 days, 20-100 people, intensive

**Structure:**
- **Day 1:**
  - Kickoff & Team Formation (2h)
  - Hacking (6h)
  - Dinner & Evening Hacking (optional)
- **Day 2:**
  - Morning Hacking (4h)
  - Presentations (2h)
  - Celebration & Prizes (1h)

**Best For:**
- Building features quickly
- Onboarding new contributors
- Experimenting with new ideas

**Budget:** $1,000-5,000 (venue, food, prizes)

### 3. Conferences

**Format:** 1-3 days, 100-1000+ people, structured

**Structure:**
- Keynotes (inspire and align)
- Technical talks (deep dives)
- Workshops (hands-on learning)
- Lightning talks (quick insights)
- Hallway track (unstructured networking)

**Best For:**
- Annual gathering
- Major announcements
- Ecosystem building

**Budget:** $10,000-100,000+ (venue, speakers, production)

### 4. Office Hours

**Format:** 1 hour, virtual, open Q&A

**Structure:**
- Drop-in video call
- No agenda (bring questions)
- Maintainers available to help
- Recorded and shared

**Best For:**
- Regular support and connection
- Newcomer onboarding
- Building relationships

**Budget:** $0 (volunteer time)

### 5. Sacred Gatherings

**Format:** 2-4 hours, 10-30 people, intentional

**Structure:**
- Opening Circle (intentions, presence)
- Deep Work or Discussion
- Reflection and Integration
- Closing (gratitude, commitments)

**Best For:**
- Strategic planning
- Conflict resolution
- Values alignment
- Community healing

**Budget:** $200-1,000 (special venue, facilitation)

## üìÖ Planning Timeline

### 12 Weeks Before

- [ ] **Define goals** - What's the purpose?
- [ ] **Choose date** - Check conflicts, holidays
- [ ] **Book venue** - Or choose virtual platform
- [ ] **Create budget** - Estimate costs, find funding
- [ ] **Assemble team** - Who will help organize?

### 8 Weeks Before

- [ ] **Open registration** - Eventbrite, form, or website
- [ ] **Invite speakers** - Reach out to potential speakers
- [ ] **Plan schedule** - Draft agenda
- [ ] **Announce event** - Social media, email, Discord

### 4 Weeks Before

- [ ] **Confirm speakers** - Finalize talks and workshops
- [ ] **Order food** - If in-person
- [ ] **Test tech** - Video, audio, streaming if virtual
- [ ] **Prepare materials** - Name tags, signs, handouts

### 1 Week Before

- [ ] **Send reminder** - Email all registered attendees
- [ ] **Finalize logistics** - Venue access, setup time
- [ ] **Brief volunteers** - Roles and responsibilities
- [ ] **Prepare opening remarks**

### 1 Day Before

- [ ] **Venue walk-through** - Know the space
- [ ] **Print materials** - Name tags, signs, schedules
- [ ] **Pack supplies** - Markers, tape, chargers
- [ ] **Get rest** - You'll need energy!

### Event Day

- [ ] **Arrive early** - Setup, tech check
- [ ] **Welcome attendees** - Friendly first impression
- [ ] **Enforce Code of Conduct** - Safety for all
- [ ] **Document** - Photos, videos (with permission)
- [ ] **Adapt** - Things will go differently than planned
- [ ] **Celebrate** - You did it!

### After Event

- [ ] **Thank participants** - Email within 24h
- [ ] **Share recordings** - If applicable
- [ ] **Publish notes** - Key takeaways
- [ ] **Send survey** - Gather feedback
- [ ] **Retrospective** - What to improve?

## ‚ôø Accessibility & Inclusion

### Venue Accessibility

**Essential:**
- Wheelchair accessible entrance and restrooms
- Elevators if multi-floor
- Clear signage
- Quiet space for sensory breaks

**Considerations:**
- Public transit accessible
- Parking available
- Gender-neutral bathrooms
- Prayer/meditation space

### Dietary Accommodations

- Ask about dietary needs in registration
- Provide vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free options
- Label all food clearly (allergens)
- Have snacks suitable for various diets

### Virtual Accessibility

- Captions/subtitles on all videos
- Screen reader compatible platforms
- Materials shared in advance
- Recording available afterward

### Schedule Accessibility

- Breaks every 60-90 minutes
- Start/end times respect timezones
- No late-night sessions (burnout)
- Option to participate asynchronously

## üé§ Running Great Events

### Opening Well

**First 10 Minutes Set the Tone:**

1. **Welcome** - Warmly greet everyone
2. **Land Acknowledgment** - Honor indigenous lands
3. **Code of Conduct** - Review briefly, share reporting process
4. **Logistics** - Bathrooms, emergency exits, WiFi, schedule
5. **Set Intentions** - Why are we here? What do we hope for?

### Facilitation Tips

**For Discussions:**
- Use talking stick or hand raises (prevent interruptions)
- "Step up, step back" (those talking a lot make space, quiet folks speak up)
- Parking lot for off-topic tangents
- Time box discussions (keeps energy)

**For Q&A:**
- Repeat questions for virtual attendees
- Take questions from chat/virtual first (often ignored)
- Balance multiple questioners (not 5 in a row from same person)
- "Let's hear from someone we haven't heard from yet"

**For Workshops:**
- Hands-on practice > passive listening
- Pair people (learning together)
- Walk around helping (not just teaching from front)
- Celebrate attempts and mistakes

### Code of Conduct Enforcement

**Be Proactive:**
- Review CoC in opening
- Visible reporting process
- Designated responders identified

**If Issues Arise:**
1. Document what happened
2. Talk to reporter privately
3. Talk to reported person privately  
4. Determine appropriate response
5. Follow up with both parties
6. See [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

## üí∞ Budget & Sponsorship

### Typical Event Costs

**Local Meetup ($100-500):**
- Venue: $0-200 (coffee shop, library, sponsor office)
- Food: $100-300 (pizza, drinks, snacks)
- Materials: $0-50 (name tags, markers)

**Hackathon ($1,000-5,000):**
- Venue: $500-2,000
- Food: $500-2,000 (2 days, 3 meals)
- Prizes: $0-500 (avoid making it about prizes)
- Materials: $100-300
- T-shirts: $300-500 (optional)

**Small Conference ($10,000-30,000):**
- Venue: $3,000-10,000
- Food: $3,000-8,000
- Speaker travel: $2,000-5,000
- Production: $1,000-3,000 (AV, streaming)
- Marketing: $500-2,000
- Misc: $500-2,000

### Finding Sponsors

**Potential Sponsors:**
- Companies using your projects
- Related tool/service companies
- Local tech companies
- Hosting providers
- Tech-friendly local businesses

**Sponsorship Tiers:**
```

- Logo on all materials

- Social media mentions

ü•à Silver ($2,000-5,000)
```

## üåê Virtual Event Best Practices

### Platform Choice

**For Small (<30 people):**
- Zoom (reliable, good breakouts)
- Discord (community familiar with it)
- Google Meet (easy access)

**For Large (>30 people):**
- Zoom Webinar (up to 10,000)
- YouTube Live (unlimited, but less interactive)
- Hopin/Airmeet (purpose-built conference platforms)

### Virtual Engagement

**Encourage Participation:**
- Polls and quizzes
- Chat discussions (assign chat moderator)
- Breakout rooms (smaller conversations)
- Collaborative documents
- Virtual backgrounds (fun theme)

**Combat Zoom Fatigue:**
- Shorter sessions (45 min not 60)
- Frequent breaks
- Camera-optional policy
- Movement breaks
- Asynchronous options

### Technical Setup

**For Speakers:**
- Test beforehand
- Good microphone (not laptop mic)
- Stable internet (wired > WiFi)
- Quiet space
- Good lighting (face camera)
- Backup device ready

**For Hosts:**
- Co-host for tech support
- Streaming backup (record locally)
- Chat moderator
- Admission controls (prevent Zoombombing)

### During Event

**Capture:**
- Photos (ask permission, especially for faces)
- Key quotes from talks
- Questions and discussions
- Whiteboard/flip chart content

**Don't:**
- Film constantly (creepy, reduces presence)
- Post photos without permission
- Share private/sensitive discussions
- Lose sight of actually being present

## üéì Event Types in Detail

### Hosting Effective Office Hours

**Preparation:**
- Announce time/link 1 week in advance
- No agenda needed
- Have latest project updates ready
- Prepare common Q&A

**During:**
- Record (announce at start)
- Welcome each person as they join
- Let conversation flow naturally
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Share screen to demonstrate
- Connect people who have similar questions

**After:**
- Share recording and notes
- Follow up on unresolved questions
- Note common issues for documentation

### Running Productive Hackathons

**Before:**
- Curate good first issues
- Prepare development environment guides
- Have mentors assigned to help
- Clear judging criteria (if applicable)

**During:**
- Form balanced teams (mix experienced + new)
- Check in regularly (are people stuck?)
- Provide mentorship, not just supervision
- Feed people well (hungry hackers = grumpy hackers)
- Mid-point demos (build excitement)

**After:**
- Showcase all projects (not just "winners")
- Merge quality PRs immediately
- Follow up on incomplete work
- Celebrate participation

## üíö Sacred Gathering Practices

**For Consciousness-First Events:**

### Opening Circle

1. Land acknowledgment
2. Moment of silence or breathing
3. Intentions (why are we here?)
4. Guidelines (speaking from "I", listening fully)

### During

- Hold space for difficult emotions
- Balance talking and listening
- Allow silence (it's generative)
- Notice and name group dynamics

### Closing Circle

1. Reflections (what are you taking away?)
2. Appreciations (specific gratitude)
3. Commitments (what will you do differently?)
4. Closing ritual (breath, poem, song)
